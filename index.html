Survey Results – Plumbers

№ Respondent Name Hypothesis Questions to Test the Hypothesis Respondents’ Answers Confirmed / Refuted Comments / Actions
1 Azamat Ibraimov It’s more convenient for plumbers to communicate with clients via messenger apps (WhatsApp/Telegram) or phone calls rather than a built-in site chat. 1) How would you prefer clients to contact you — phone call, messenger, or site chat?
2) Would you like the site messages to be forwarded to Telegram/WhatsApp?
3) Do you need chat history stored on the platform?		 “Messenger or phone is best. I always reply faster on Telegram — a built-in chat is fine only if it forwards messages. Otherwise I might miss them.” Confirmed (partially) Add quick-contact buttons (“Write in Telegram/WhatsApp”), push or messenger notifications, and optional built-in chat linked to messengers.
2 Baurzhan Sarsenov Partial prepayment (deposit) increases reliability and is convenient for plumbers. 1) Do you usually ask for partial prepayment?
2) What percentage seems fair?
3) Are cancellations or non-payment a problem?		 “Yes, I take 20–30% in advance for big jobs. For small ones — full payment after. The worst is when clients cancel at the last minute. A deposit helps avoid that.” Confirmed Add an option “partial prepayment (materials/deposit)” in job requests, default 20–30%. Implement a safe refund/dispute system.
3 Ernis Zholdoshev Plumbers are willing to pay a small platform commission if it provides stable job flow and payment guarantees. 1) Would you pay a commission?
2) Prefer a flat fee or % of the order?
3) What do you expect in return?		 “If I get more steady orders — yes, 5–10% is OK. But a fixed 200 KGS for a small job is too high. I want guarantees of payment and help with disputes.” Confirmed (conditionally) Create a flexible model: low % for small jobs, optional premium plan with lower fee & priority. Explain fees clearly.
4 Tursunbek Mamatov Plumbers prefer a simple cost calculator or quote template to send estimates to clients. 1) Do you use cost estimate templates?
2) Would a built-in calculator for materials/work be useful?
3) Do you want to send quotes through the platform?		 “I calculate in my head or notebook. A calculator would help, especially for new guys. Sending a quote directly from the platform saves time.” Confirmed Add a simple estimate builder (materials, labor, transport) and “Send quote to client” button. Allow saving templates.
5 Saltanat Akmatova Verified profiles (ID / certificates) and ratings build client trust and benefit plumbers. 1) Would you verify your ID or upload certificates?
2) Would a high rating help attract clients?
3) Are you afraid of bad reviews?		 “Yes, verification is good — people trust it. Ratings help too, but one bad review can ruin the profile. I’d like a way to reply or appeal.” Confirmed Add optional “Verified” badge, allow responses to reviews, add moderation/dispute system.
6 Kamchy Osmonov Most plumbers prefer small, quick jobs as their main income source rather than long projects. 1) What gives you most of your income — small quick jobs or long projects?
2) How many hours per week can you spend on the platform?		 “Mostly quick fixes — you can do more in a day. Big projects take too long. I can work 6–8 hours daily, mostly evenings.” Confirmed Split orders into “Quick Jobs” and “Projects.” Add filters by duration/price. Allow plumbers to choose job type preference.
7 Nurbek Satybaldiev Flexible scheduling (weekend/evening availability) helps plumbers get more orders. 1) Would you like to set your working hours?
2) Would showing “available on weekends/evenings” help you get more clients?
3) Should the system allow time slots/booking?		 “Yes, schedule is important. If clients see I work evenings, they book me more often. Auto-booking is OK if it’s simple.” Confirmed Add work schedule in profile, filters for “available weekends/evenings,” optional time-slot booking system.
